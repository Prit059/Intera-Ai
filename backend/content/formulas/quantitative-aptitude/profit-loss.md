# Profit & Loss 

## Overview
Master profit and loss concepts with formulas, shortcuts, and real-world examples. Essential for placement exams like TCS, Infosys, Wipro, and banking aptitude tests.

## Key Formulas


### Profit Formula
```latex
Profit = SP - CP
```

**Explanation:** Profit occurs when Selling Price (SP) is greater than Cost Price (CP). Profit = SP - CP


### Loss Formula
```latex
Loss = CP - SP
```

**Explanation:** Loss occurs when Cost Price (CP) is greater than Selling Price (SP). Loss = CP - SP


## Examples


### Example 1
**Question:** A shopkeeper buys an article for ₹500 and sells it for ₹600. Find the profit percentage.
**Solution:** Profit = SP - CP = 600 - 500 = ₹100\nProfit% = (Profit/CP) × 100 = (100/500) × 100 = 20%


## Important Concepts
- Cost Price (CP): The price at which an article is purchased

## Common Mistakes to Avoid
- Calculating profit/loss percentage on SP instead of CP

## Tips & Shortcuts
- Always calculate profit/loss percentage on Cost Price (CP)
